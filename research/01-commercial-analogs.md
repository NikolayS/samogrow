## Per-product notes

### Auk (Auk Mini 2)
Scandinavian design brand. The Mini 2 (~$229/£199, £209 pre-order, shipping ~late-July/Aug
2026) holds **4 coco-fibre pots** and uses a **24 W** LED. Reservoir lasts ~19 days between
refills — a big jump from the original Mini. The companion app controls lighting and adds a
"holiday mode" that slows growth while you're away. **Most important for us: Auk has no
proprietary pods and no subscription — you plant your own seeds in coco fibre.** No camera/AI
vision; "smart" here means scheduling + app control. There is also a larger "Auk 1". Small
capacity is the main limitation.
Sources: [auk.com/products/auk-mini-2](https://www.auk.com/products/auk-mini-2),
[Trusted Reviews](https://www.trustedreviews.com/news/the-new-auk-mini-2-boasts-an-app-for-smart-herb-growing),
[T3](https://www.t3.com/home-living/kitchen/auk-upgrades-its-five-star-smart-garden-and-its-gotten-bigger-and-smaller-at-the-same-time),
[MacSources](https://macsources.com/auk-unveils-new-auk-mini-2/).

### Click & Grow
The mass-market countertop leader. Line: Smart Garden 3 ($99.95), 9 ($199.95), 9 Pro
($259.95), 27 ($599.95), Wall Farm ($2,499). Wicking/capillary self-watering — very low
maintenance but **fixed light height** limits crops to herbs/greens, and growth is slow.
Fully proprietary pods ($4.95 each; subscription $7.95/mo or $94.95/yr) — first-year
consumables run **$150–250**, the classic razor-and-blades trap.
Sources: [clickandgrow.com SG3](https://www.clickandgrow.com/products/the-smart-garden-3),
[SG9](https://www.clickandgrow.com/products/the-smart-garden-9),
[subscription](https://www.clickandgrow.com/pages/click-grow-subscription),
[Greener Pods review](https://greenerpods.com/2026-comprehensive-click-and-grow-review/),
[Greener Pods best-of](https://greenerpods.com/best-smart-garden/).

### AeroGarden
Announced closure effective Jan 1 2025, then **relaunched Spring 2025** backed by parent
Scotts Miracle-Gro. As of early 2026 pods are back in stock, support/warranty active, and the
app is being rebuilt. Bounty-class units ~$230–370, 6–9 plants, reservoir + circulation pump,
pods ~$120–200/yr. Buy with awareness of **brand-stability risk** and a still-recovering app.
Sources: [Vegbed closure](https://www.vegbed.com/blogs/news/aerogarden-closes-its-doors-after-twenty-years-of-innovating-indoor-gardening),
[Greener Pods 2026 review](https://greenerpods.com/aerogarden-review/),
[Business Wise](https://businesswisemag.com/is-aerogarden-going-out-of-business/).

### Rise Gardens
Furniture-style modular horizontal system. Personal $279 (8–12 plants); Rise Garden Single
$350 (12 pods), expandable to 24/36 across tiers, up to ~$1,499. Membership from $13.71/mo
(1-yr $199–299, 2-yr $329–499). Real hydroponics with app dosing guidance — **rewarding for
people who want to garden, but a steep learning curve** and active decision-making. Complaints:
seed sprouting rates, subscription variety.
Sources: [risegardens.com Rise Garden](https://risegardens.com/products/the-rise-garden),
[Personal](https://risegardens.com/products/personal-indoor-garden),

[HGTV](https://www.hgtv.com/shopping/product-reviews/rise-hydroponic-indoor-garden-review).

### Gardyn
The closest analog to an "AI-controlled" garden and the key reference for our project. Home 4
kit ~$899, **30 plants in ~2 sq ft** vertical tower. **Two onboard cameras + environmental
sensors feed the Kelby AI assistant**: vision-based plant ID, per-pod monitoring, predictive
health alerts, harvest-timing and auto grow-cycle adjustments. But the AI is **gated behind a
$29–39/mo membership (~$408/yr)** and pods are proprietary yCubes — 3-year total cost of
ownership exceeds $2,500.
Sources: [mygardyn.com Home Kit](https://mygardyn.com/product/gardyn-home-kit/),
[membership](https://mygardyn.com/gardyn-membership/),
[Greener Pods 11-month review](https://greenerpods.com/gardyn-review-11-months-of-testing-the-849-vertical-garden-2026/),
[Smart Home Explorer](https://www.smarthomeexplorer.com/guides/best-smart-greenhouse-grow-systems-2026).

### Plantui
Finnish, design-award (Red Dot) system. SG3 (3 plants, 1 L) and SG6 (6 plants, 3 L; up to 12
with pre-grow tray, or microgreens). ~$242 (SG) / ~€265 (~$300) for the 6. Periodic
flood/drain hydroponics, proprietary capsules, minimal app — premium price for small capacity,
mostly EU availability.
Sources: [plantui.io SG6](https://plantui.io/product/smart-garden-6/),
[review](https://lennartwoermer.de/en/2025/02/plantui-smart-garden-review/).

### LetPot
Strong value/mid-range. LPH-Max $251.99: **21 pods (+2 large)**, **36 W** 8-level dimmable LED
(extends to 30"), auto drip irrigation with water-level sensor, 7.5 L tank (~30 days). App +
WiFi control and an LCD screen; 4.6★. SE ~$80–100 (12 pods), Air (10 pods). **Open seed system
— no proprietary pod lock-in.** Main knock: newer, less-proven brand.
Sources: [letpot.com LPH-Max](https://letpot.com/products/lph-max-21-pod-hydroponics-system-with-app-control),
[She She Grows review](https://shershegrows.com/letpot-lph-max-review/),
[Gardening Know How](https://www.gardeningknowhow.com/edible/vegetables/letpot-lph-max-review).

### iDOO
Budget champion. 12-pod under $80 (deals £55–65), 20-pod version too. **Open seed system, no
subscription.** Reservoir + pump, top-ups every 1–2 weeks. Complaints: **noisy/gurgling pump**,
thin plastic, unclear instructions, missing accessories, basic/no smart app. Delivers
surprising value despite compromises.
Sources: [Jungle Hugger review](https://junglehugger.com/idoo-hydroponics-growing-system/),
[Ponics Life](https://ponicslife.com/idoo-hydroponics-growing-system-review-12-pod/),
[idooworld.com](https://www.idooworld.com/products/home-garden-kit).

## Lessons for our DIY build

1. **The consumables/subscription trap is the whole game.** Every premium brand monetizes
   proprietary pods and/or a mandatory membership: Click & Grow $150–250/yr, Gardyn ~$408/yr
   AI subscription, Lettuce Grow $200–400/yr. A DIY build using **rockwool/coco cubes + bulk
   seeds + generic 2-part nutrients costs a few dollars per grow cycle** and is our single
   biggest structural advantage. Design explicitly around open, commodity consumables.

2. **Price target.** Mid-range app-controlled units land at **$200–260** (Auk Mini 2 $229,
   LetPot Max $252, Click & Grow SG9 $200). The AI-camera tier (Gardyn) is $899 + subscription.
   To beat the market, keep our all-in hardware BOM **under ~$200** while delivering the AI
   monitoring only Gardyn offers — and with **zero subscription**.

3. **AI is the real differentiator, and only Gardyn has it.** A **camera + sensors feeding a
   vision model** (plant ID, per-plant health, harvest timing, failure detection) is the
   headline feature people pay $899 + $408/yr for. This is exactly where a local/LLM-driven DIY
   build can undercut them — same capability, no recurring fee. Prioritize a camera and a few
   cheap sensors (water level, temp/humidity, maybe EC/pH).

4. **Match the features that matter, skip the ones that don't.**
   - Must-have: automated watering with a **water-level sensor** and a multi-week reservoir
     (LetPot 30 days, Auk 19 days set the bar); **app control + scheduling**; **dimmable
     full-spectrum LED** (~24–36 W for a countertop unit).
   - High-value: camera-based monitoring/AI, holiday/vacation mode (slow growth while away).
   - Skip: proprietary pod ecosystems, over-designed enclosures.

5. **Avoid the known pain points competitors ship with:** fixed light height that blocks tall
   crops (Click & Grow) — make our **light height adjustable**; noisy pumps (iDOO) — pick a
   quiet pump or wicking; buggy/immature apps (AeroGarden) and steep learning curves (Rise) —
   keep the UX and dosing guidance simple and automated.

6. **Capacity sweet spot is ~6–20 plants.** Countertop herb units cluster at 3–9 (Auk 4,
   C&G 9); value hydroponic units at 12–21 (iDOO, LetPot). For a herb garden, **~9–12 plants**
   is a sensible target — enough variety without Gardyn-scale footprint.

7. **Brand/longevity is a trust factor we sidestep.** AeroGarden's shutdown-and-relaunch shows
   how proprietary-pod owners get stranded when a vendor wavers. An **open, self-serviceable
   DIY design with commodity parts** is inherently more durable — worth emphasizing as a design
   value.
