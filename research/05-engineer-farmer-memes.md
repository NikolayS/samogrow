# Engineer-to-Farmer Meme Genre — Research

Research for positioning the samogrow kit toward software engineers by referencing the "tech career arc ends in farming" meme genre. Compiled 2026-07-05.

## Summary

The "engineer secretly dreams of quitting to farm" trope is real, well-documented, and beloved in programmer communities. The single most canonical, viral anchor is the **Feng Yuan / Microsoft "goose farmer" story (2024)** — a real principal engineer whose LinkedIn title change to "Goose Farmer" went viral and spawned a wave of image macros. Around it sits a genre of essays and forum threads ("Why do programmers want to be farmers?", "cottagecore programmers", HN "learn to weld"). Below are exact-text, attributed, linkable instances plus a usage/copyright assessment.

## 1. Viral posts and canonical instances

### A. The Microsoft "Goose Farmer" (Feng Yuan) — the canonical anchor
- **What it is:** Feng Yuan spent 22 years at Microsoft (Principal Performance Architect / Principal Software Development Engineer; worked on Azure, Office 365, Bing). After being laid off in July 2023, his LinkedIn job title read **"Goose Farmer."** A tweet screenshotting the LinkedIn change went viral in spring 2024.
- **Originating tweet:** Smarika Malviya, handle **@smarika_17** (Twitter/X), ~April 2024. The article coverage does not preserve the exact tweet wording, so quote the *story*, not a fabricated tweet line.
- **Most-quotable attributed repost (exact text):**
  > "this developer retired or resigned from software giant after 22 years 4 mos, and became a farmer. bro is living his dream"
  — **@nixcraft** (Vivek Gite, a large, well-known Linux/tech account), Threads, 2024-04-30. Engagement: ~3.2K likes, 273 reposts, 48 comments.
  URL: https://www.threads.com/@nixcraft/post/C6Zvz6cyg9I
- **Nuance to keep honest:** Yuan later clarified he was terminated ("low performance"), not that he voluntarily quit, and had started raising geese for personal food a year earlier. So the "quit tech to farm" framing is partly meme mythology layered on a real layoff. If we reference it, frame it as "the story the internet turned into a meme," not literal fact.
- **Coverage / citations:**
  - 80.lv, "Microsoft Developer Fired After 22 Years, Becomes a Goose Farmer," 2024-05-02: https://80.lv/articles/microsoft-developer-fired-after-22-years-becomes-a-goose-farmer
  - Market Realist coverage: https://marketrealist.com/why-did-this-man-choose-goose-farming-over-his-job-at-microsoft/
- **Virality:** Strong. Multiple outlets, multiple large accounts, and it seeded a persistent image-macro genre (below).

## 2. Cultural datapoints / analysis pieces (for making the positioning land)

These are the best "this is a real phenomenon" citations — essays that name and analyze the trope.

1. **"Why do so many programmers want to be farmers?"** — Michael Graham, EngineerDog.com, 2023-05-29. Core argument (quotable):
   > Gardening is "100% physical work. The outputs are tangible. Problems encountered are all straight forward. Basically the exact opposite of coding."
   Coding is "mentally exhausting yet physically sedentary"; gardening is the counterbalance. Cites *Clarkson's Farm* as cultural validation.
   URL: https://engineerdog.com/2023/05/29/why-do-so-many-programmers-want-to-be-farmers-how-to-build-a-corrugated-steel-garden-box/

2. **"Cottagecore Programmers: The Idealization of Farming by Tech"** — Theodore Morley, tjmorley.com. Thesis: tech workers romanticize agrarian/craft labor out of alienation from abstract work and a mythologized past. Cites HN threads, r/cscareerquestions, r/sysadmin escape-to-farming discussions. Good "meta" citation that names the whole genre.
   URL: https://tjmorley.com/blogposts/cottagecoreprogrammers.html

3. **"How I became a homestead software engineer"** — Eytan Avisror, Medium, Oct 2022. First-person: bi-yearly burnout → bought 5 acres in East Tennessee, kept working remotely while homesteading. Good "you don't have to fully quit — you can do both" datapoint (aligns with a DIY-garden kit, not literal career exit).
   URL: https://medium.com/@eytan_avisror/how-i-became-a-homestead-software-engineer-853a506a9abd

4. **Burnout stat (use with caution / re-verify before publishing):** The YouTube piece "How Farming Saved Me From Developer Burnout" cites "81% of developers have experienced burnout." I could not confirm the underlying survey — treat as unverified secondhand; if we want a burnout stat on the page, source a primary survey (e.g., a named developer-wellbeing report) rather than this.

## 3. Copyright / practical embedding assessment

| Item | Safe to QUOTE text + attribute + link? | Re-host image? | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| @nixcraft Threads post | Yes — short quote, clear attribution + link = fair-use-friendly | No | Quote the one-liner, link the post |
| Feng Yuan goose-farmer story | Yes — it's a widely-reported news event; cite outlets | No (don't screenshot LinkedIn) | Reference the story, link 80.lv |
| ProgrammerHumor macros | Describe in your own words + link | **No** — aggregator, unknown original author/rights | Link out, don't embed |
| EngineerDog / tjmorley essays | Yes — short quoted lines with attribution | N/A | Quote 1 line each, link |
| HN threads | Yes — public, linkable | N/A | Link only |

**General rule for the project page:** Quoting a sentence or two with author name + live link is low-risk and is what we should do. Re-hosting anyone's meme image (especially aggregator-sourced macros of unknown authorship) is the thing to avoid — link out instead, or commission/generate our own original riff on the format.
